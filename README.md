The Stanford Heart Transplant data contains the survival of patients on the waiting list of the Stanford
heart transplant program. Here, we analyze the survival of 103 patients on the waiting list for
the Stanford heart transplant program. The purpose of this report is to outline the results of the
survival analysis on the heart transplant data. Analyses are done using Kaplan-Meier estimates and
fitting a Cox-proportional hazards model. The Kaplan-Meier estimates show the survival time of
individuals who have received prior bypass surgery are higher in comparison to those who have not
had a history of bypass surgery. Additionally, using the Kaplan-Meier estimate we see that those
who have received a heart transplant have a higher survival time compared to individuals who have
not. Furthermore, from the Cox-proportional hazards model, we conclude that the transplant variable
is indeed time-dependent, and there are interactions between factors in the data, specifically
between year and transplant, which must be included into the Cox-proportional hazard model.
